Picking Parts for a PC

There are many factors to consider when you're shopping for PC parts. The biggest and most important question is "what is my PC going to be used for?" Am I using it for video editing, 3d modeling, gaming, or word processing? This question will help determine what parts to prioritize.

Here are some basic guidelines. Am I going to be...

  • Gaming - Requires a fast graphics card and less on your CPU and RAM.
  • Video Editing - Requires a good CPU and lots of RAM plus storage.
  • 3D Modeling - Needs a good GPU and CPU with more RAM than a usual gaming PC.
  • Word Processing - Doesn't need a strong computer, so spend more money on a good keyboard.

These are all very different PCs that fit each use case better. Deciding this early on will help you get the computer you actually need.

The next most important question is budget. How much are you able to spend and additionally, how much should you spend? There's no need to spend more to get performance that's not going to help you. For example, a person who just does internet browsing and word processing doesn't need the best of the best CPU out there. Something that's a tenth the price could have served them just as well.
